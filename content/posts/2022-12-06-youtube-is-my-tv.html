When we bought our house in 2015 we got all the regular communication appliances; TVs + cable, landlines, internet, etc.
But we soon realized that we prefer to be on our computers more and as such the TV was left unused, most of the time.

Recently I realized that I'm really using YouTube as if it was my cable TV. I could also clear see the type of content that I'm watching, and it made me think that I've actually built my own TV channel.

In order to share some gems I wanted to write this blogpost.

Marques Brownlee has been at the game for quite a while, he does a bunch of hardware tech reviews and has recently even ventured into car reviews, mostly EVs.

Undecided with Matt Ferrell is a channel about undecided technologies, solutions etc. Matt is always coming with new information from the STEM field and I find this quite interesting.

Joe Scott has more of a science channel, but does quite a broad range of topics. His work is always quite researched, and he has a very calm and soothing voice.

Wendover Productions is a channel about aviation and transportation, at heart. However, he also does a lot of other interesting topics, like China's energy grid, Angola's richest city or NYCs water supply.

Honorable mentions: Andrew Rousso, Lennar Young, Drew Lynch, Elanor Morton, Chris Turner, CalebCity, Fairbairn Films.

Vlog Creations is practical comedy in Florida. So that's absurdity on top of absurdity. Motorized porta-potties, family van with hydraulics, dressing up as a spider and calling pest control. All with a good heart, which must be said!

Sammy O'Beid is a Palestian-American comedian. He used to be a math teacher, so there's quite a few math-related jokes involved. Obviously doesn't shy away from making fun/drawing attention to his heritage.

Chocolate Sundaes is a predominantly black and people of color comedy channel. They're part of the Laugh Factory company. Some comedians feel like they're not even doing a bit, they're just sharing stories and they're funny as hell.

Trevor Wallace is a stand-up comedian, although also quite a YouTube personality. He's very good at "fuck boi", "crypto bro", "Subaru driver" and similar stereotypes. Somehow he always makes it look so natural and if you watch his performance videos also, clearly good at crowd-work.

Gaming

{{< img src="/img/blog/yt-tv/levelcapgaming.webp" desc="LevelCapGaming" url="https://www.youtube.com/@levelcapgaming" >}}
{{< img src="/img/blog/yt-tv/quill18.webp" desc="Quill18" url="https://www.youtube.com/@quill18" >}}
{{< img src="/img/blog/yt-tv/katherineofsky.webp" desc="Katherine of Sky" url="https://www.youtube.com/@katherineofsky" >}}
{{< img src="/img/blog/yt-tv/splattercatgaming.webp" desc="SplatterCatGaming" url="https://www.youtube.com/@splattercatgaming" >}}

LevelCapGaming is a big name in the First-Person-Shooter (FPS) community. He's been documenting the Battlefield franchise since I can remember. He's also doing news updates from time to time, very useful for someone who doesn't play many FPSs anymore.

Quill18 is a Canadian YouTuber who's big into 4X, but also space games and colony builders. He used to be a programmer and has a second channel where he does programming tutorials for games, as well as videos of him attending Game Jams such as Ludum Dare.

KatherineOfSky is big into productivity and management games. She's also a big fan of anything cute. Her biggest series is Factorio. I consider her the Queen of Blueprints.

SplatterCatGaming does all the indies games that come out. He's perfected it to a tee, and within a 30-minute video you can get a good overview of what's in the game. There's a lot of research and playing that's required.
